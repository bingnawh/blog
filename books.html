My favourites, and my book journey.

I'm commiting to reading a book a week until I hit 50 books. I will be listing down the books I read, in which week, and a short sentence or two about the books I read.

  • When Breath Becomes Air by Paul Kalanithi
  • The Sailor Who Fell from Grace with the Sea by Yukio Mishima: Beautifully written prose hidden with metaphors which makes a lot more sense if you understand the life and beliefs of Yukio Mishima.
  • Ultralearning by Scott H. Young
  • The Lean Startup by Eric Ries
  • Shoe Dog by Phil Knight
  • On The Shortness of Life by Seneca: Short yet dense with wisdom on stoic philosophy that is still relevant today.
  • Talking to Strangers by Malcolm Gladwell
  • This Is What Inequality Looks Like by You Yenn Teo: Brilliant case study into inequality in Singapore and its effects on the privileged and unprivileged.
  • In the Buddha's Words by Bhikkhu Bodhi
